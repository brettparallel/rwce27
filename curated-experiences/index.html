Match Day Hospitality Licence Fee for OTAs

The Match Day Hospitality Licence Fee is a commercial framework that allows Official Travel Agents (OTAs) to host and deliver pre-match hospitality functions as part of their travel packages for Rugby World Cup events. Under this model, OTAs are granted the right to independently create and manage hospitality experiences, while paying a per-person licence fee to RWCE.

Fee Structure

The licence fee is applied on a per-person basis, varying by tournament stage:

  • Pool Stage Matches: $50 per person
  • Knockout Matches: $70 per person

These fees are payable to RWCE for each guest attending the OTA’s hospitality function.

Commercial Model
  • The licence fee is typically based on an agreed minimum attendance.
  • If actual attendance exceeds the agreed minimum, additional guests are charged at a flat per-head rate.
  • RWCE will invoice the OTA for the licence fee based on:
    • The confirmed minimum (initial invoice), and
    • Any post-event reconciliation for additional attendees.
OTA Responsibilities

Under this model, OTAs operate with a high degree of independence and responsibility:

  • Direct Supplier Engagement:
    OTAs must contract directly with venues, caterers, and other suppliers following introduction by RWCE.
  • Event Delivery:
    OTAs are fully responsible for:
    • Event planning and execution
    • Staffing and contractors
    • Health & safety compliance
    • Required licences and insurance
  • Customer Management:
    All customer service, complaints, cancellations, and refunds are managed solely by the OTA.
  • Legal & Compliance:
    OTAs must comply with all applicable laws, including Australian Consumer Law, and ensure suppliers meet regulatory requirements.
Approval & Brand Control
  • All Match Day Hospitality events require prior written approval from RWCE before any supplier contracts are signed.
  • OTAs must ensure events meet the quality and brand standards of the Rugby World Cup and do not damage its reputation.
  • OTAs cannot present themselves as acting on behalf of RWCE.
Reporting & Reconciliation
  • After each event, the OTA must provide a signed post-event confirmation letter outlining final attendance numbers.
  • A formal sales report must be submitted within 14 days of the match.
  • RWCE will then issue any additional invoices based on actual attendance exceeding the minimum.
Key Limitations
  • Hospitality cannot be delivered inside official match venues.
  • The licence is non-exclusive, meaning multiple OTAs may operate similar experiences.
  • RWCE retains full control over official hospitality products and curated experiences.
Process to Proceed

If an OTA chooses to adopt this model:

  1. RWCE issues a Licence Fee amendment to the OTA agreement.
  2. RWCE introduces the OTA to relevant venue suppliers.
  3. The OTA confirms a minimum attendance number.
  4. RWCE raises the initial licence fee invoice based on that minimum.
  5. The OTA delivers the event and completes post-event reporting.
